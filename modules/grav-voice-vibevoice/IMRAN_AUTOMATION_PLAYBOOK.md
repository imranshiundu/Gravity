# VibeVoice - Imran Automation Playbook

## What this is
Private archival/workbench clone of Microsoft's `VibeVoice` project for future voice AI integration work.

## Why it matters
VibeVoice is relevant as a future asset for:
- real-time voice interaction
- streaming TTS
- voice-agent UX
- spoken assistant output
- future telephony / live voice workflows

## Important reality
- The original long-form `VibeVoice-TTS` code path was removed by Microsoft.
- The most relevant surviving direction for us is the real-time / streaming voice capability and the broader voice-AI stack around it.
- This is **not** an immediate survival priority ahead of the revenue / continuity target.

## How we may use it later
1. Experiment with a spoken Haico interface
2. Evaluate voice latency and deployment cost
3. Test future call/assistant workflows
4. Compare it against simpler TTS stacks before production choices

## Business value lens
Before spending real time on this, we should ask:
- Does it help us make money faster?
- Does it reduce cost enough to matter?
- Does it improve product UX enough to justify the complexity?

If not, it stays in the future-capability bucket.
